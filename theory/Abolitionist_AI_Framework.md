# 🧠 Abolitionist AI: Beyond Reformist Ethics

> "AI ethics often stops at making algorithms 'less biased.' Abolitionist AI asks why the algorithm exists in the first place."

Abolitionist AI is a framework for dismantling oppressive technological systems rather than merely polishing them. It draws from the history of abolitionist movements to challenge the proliferation of AI in policing, surveillance, and border control.

## 🏛️ CORE PRINCIPLES

### 1. Reject Reformism
Reformist AI ethics seeks to "fix" biased algorithms (e.g., making facial recognition more accurate for Black faces). Abolitionist AI recognizes that an accurate surveillance tool is still a surveillance tool. We seek to **decommission** rather than **debias**.

### 2. Shift Power, Not Just Values
While "fairness" and "transparency" are desirable, they are often used for "participation-washing." Abolitionist AI focuses on **Power**: Who owns the data? Who controls the model? Who has the agency to say "No"?

### 3. Prefigurative Direct Action
Don't wait for a law to change. Use tools that enable resistance **now**:
*   **Adversarial Defense**: Using tools like *Glaze* or *Nightshade* to poison data and protect creators.
*   **Data Withholding**: Collectively bargaining by withholding data from corporate oligopolies.
*   **Mutual Aid Networks**: Building decentralized AI that serves communities directly, bypassing corporate gatekeepers.

## ✊ TACTICAL APPLICATIONS
*   **Abolitionist Design**: Building policing alternatives and restorative justice mechanisms that don't rely on carceral logic.
*   **Decentralized Governance**: Moving from top-down corporate scraping to active, informed consent and community-owned datasets.
